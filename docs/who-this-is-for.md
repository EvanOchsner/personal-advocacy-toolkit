# Who this is for

*(Stub — filled in Phase 5. Working notes only.)*

## Primary audience

Non-technical people organizing a fact-heavy dispute so that a regulator,
advocate, journalist, or attorney can act on it effectively. You are not
expected to understand git, hashes, or YAML going in — the tutorials walk
you through what each tool does and why.

## Explicit non-goals

- **Not for** becoming your own lawyer.
- **Not for** automating a pro se filing to the point where you don't need
  counsel.
- **Not for** evidence-gathering in criminal matters — the chain-of-custody
  model here is designed for *civil* / *regulatory* contexts; criminal
  evidentiary standards are stricter and should involve law enforcement.

## Secondary audience

Civic-legal-tech practitioners evaluating the mechanics for integration
with their platforms: Suffolk LIT Lab (Document Assembly Line), LSC TIG
grantees, United Policyholders, legal-aid tech staff.
