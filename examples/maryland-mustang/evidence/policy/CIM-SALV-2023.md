Salvage and Total Loss Provisions

## Section 1 — Total Loss Declaration

1.1 A vehicle is declared a total loss where the cost of repair plus
residual value exceeds the Agreed Value (for Agreed-Value policies)
or the Actual Cash Value (for ACV policies), or where repair is not
feasible.

## Section 2 — Settlement Check

2.1 Upon declaration of total loss, the insurer tenders a settlement
check in the amount of indemnity. Acceptance of the settlement check,
without reservation of rights in writing, constitutes release of the
claim.

## Section 3 — Salvage Transfer

3.1 Transfer of the vehicle to the insurer's salvage vendor may
occur only upon:

    (a) delivery of a signed release from the insured, completing the
        total-loss settlement; **or**

    (b) written consent of the insured authorizing transfer prior to
        settlement.

3.2 Absent (a) or (b) above, the vehicle shall remain at the
repair facility or at a location designated by the insured,
pending resolution.

3.3 A salvage transfer effected in violation of §3.1 is ultra vires
and does not prejudice the insured's right to pursue the full Agreed
Value.

## Section 4 — Insured's Right to Retain

4.1 The insured may elect to retain the salvage vehicle by accepting
a reduced settlement equal to the Agreed Value less salvage value.
Election shall be in writing.
