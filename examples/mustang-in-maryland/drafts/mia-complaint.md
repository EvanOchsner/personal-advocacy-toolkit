## I. Summary

Chesapeake Indemnity Mutual ("CIM") has applied a $5,280.50 deduction
labeled "non-customary charges" against the Agreed Value of
Complainant's 1969 Ford Mustang Mach 1, insured under an Agreed Value
endorsement (CIM-AV-ENDT-2023), and on June 24, 2025 transferred the
vehicle to its salvage vendor while Complainant's written objection
was on file and no release had been signed. Complainant requests MIA
review of two issues:

1. **Unauthorized salvage transfer in violation of CIM-SALV-2023 §3.**
2. **Improper substitution of a vendor ACV for the Agreed Value, and
   improper re-characterization of a Specialist Shop's published rate
   schedule as "non-customary."**

## II. Facts

1. On March 15, 2025, Complainant's 1969 Ford Mustang Mach 1 was
   rear-ended at a stoplight in Columbia, MD. Complainant was not at
   fault. (Howard County PD report CPD-SYN-2025-0315-0042.)

2. On March 16, 2025, Complainant filed a first notice of loss with
   CIM. Claim CIM-2025-03-5517 opened.

3. On March 19, 2025, Dundalk Bodywork and Crabcakes — a
   general-population body shop in Baltimore County — declined the
   repair, citing parts-sourcing constraints for the 1969 platform
   and lack of a frame jig for vintage work.

4. On March 21, 2025, CIM adjuster Harlan Whitlock approved movement
   of the vehicle to Midlife Crisis Restorations of Wilmington, a vintage
   specialist in Wilmington, DE, for inspection. CIM's written
   approval planted the "customary-rate" limitation in advance of
   any written rate-schedule objection.

5. On March 24, 2025, Midlife Crisis Restorations forwarded its **published rate
   schedule** to CIM by email with the adjuster in CC: $95/hr
   vintage body labor, $55/day storage after day 7, parts sourcing
   cost + 15%. **CIM did not object in writing.** Under CIM-VEH-2023
   §7.3, failure to object within 14 days constitutes
   acknowledgement of the schedule for this claim.

6. On April 7, 2025, MidAtlantic Vehicle Appraisers ("MAVA"), a
   vendor retained by CIM, inspected the vehicle at Midlife Crisis Restorations.

7. On April 17, 2025, MAVA issued report MAVA-2025-04-0117, producing
   an ACV of $52,719.50 and a repair estimate of $34,802.00 at
   "customary regional rates" of $75/hr labor — a rate inapplicable
   to vintage-platform work and not reflective of the Specialist
   Shop population contemplated by CIM-VEH-2023 Article VII.

8. On April 18, 2025, Complainant objected in writing to the MAVA
   methodology on two grounds: (a) vendor ACV cannot substitute for
   Agreed Value absent fraud or material misrepresentation under
   CIM-AV-ENDT-2023 §2, and (b) $75/hr is not the Specialist Shop
   rate.

9. On April 21, 2025, Complainant retrieved the full policy form set
   from the producing agency, Meritor Insurance Group, Annapolis MD,
   because CIM's online portal provided only the Declarations page.

10. On May 9, 2025, CIM issued a formal position letter offering
    Agreed Value minus a $5,280.50 deduction and minus the
    deductible. The deduction comprised three line items: storage
    beyond 7 days ($1,840.00), "specialist labor rate premium"
    ($2,640.50), and parts sourcing fee ($800.00).

11. On May 22, 2025, Complainant objected in writing, citing
    CIM-AV-ENDT-2023 §2(a), CIM-VEH-2023 §§7.2, 7.3, 9.1, and the
    prior unanswered rate-schedule transmission.

12. On June 10, 2025, CIM reiterated the offer as "final" and warned
    that absent signed release within 14 days the vehicle would be
    transferred to salvage under CIM-SALV-2023.

13. **On June 24, 2025, CIM transferred the vehicle to its salvage
    vendor, Tri-State Salvage Partners, Elkton MD, with no signed

## III. Issues for MIA review

### A. Unauthorized salvage transfer (lead grievance).

CIM-SALV-2023 §3 permits salvage transfer only upon (a) signed
release completing a total-loss settlement, or (b) written
pre-settlement consent of the insured. Neither occurred. The transfer
was ultra vires under §3.3 of the same form and does not prejudice
Complainant's right to pursue the full Agreed Value.

### B. Improper substitution of vendor ACV for Agreed Value.

CIM-AV-ENDT-2023 §2 forecloses substitution of a vendor ACV for the
Agreed Value absent fraud or material misrepresentation. CIM has not
alleged either. The MAVA $52,719.50 ACV is not a permissible measure
of indemnity.

### C. Improper "non-customary" characterization of Specialist Shop rates.

CIM-VEH-2023 Article VII expressly contemplates Specialist Shop
rates and defines "customary rate" relative to *equivalent specialist
work*, not general-population body-shop rate. §7.3 treats an
un-objected published schedule as acknowledged. The rate schedule
was forwarded to CIM on March 24, 2025; no objection followed within
14 days.

## IV. Relief requested

1. Payment of Agreed Value ($58,000) less $500 deductible =
   **$57,500.00**, in full satisfaction of indemnity, without the
   $5,280.50 deduction.

2. Written correction of CIM's claim file to remove the
   "non-customary charges" characterization.

3. Confirmation that the June 24, 2025 salvage transfer and any
   settlement check issued after it shall not be treated as
   constructive acceptance of the May 9 offer.
