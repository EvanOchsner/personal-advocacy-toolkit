## Article I — Definitions

1.1 **Classic Vehicle.** A vehicle 20 years or older, maintained in
a condition substantially consistent with its original manufacturer
specification, and used for limited recreational, exhibition, or
club purposes.

1.2 **Specialist Shop.** A repair facility with demonstrated
experience in period-correct restoration of the insured vehicle's
era and platform.

1.3 **Customary Rate.** A rate comparable to prevailing rates for
*equivalent work* in the region of the repair. For specialist work,
"equivalent" refers to the narrower population of specialist shops
capable of performing the work, not the general-population body-shop
rate.

## Article VII — Repair and Specialist Labor

7.1 The insurer shall pay for repair at a Specialist Shop when a
repair facility of the insured's ordinary choice has declined the
work due to platform- or era-specific scope.

7.2 Specialist labor rates shall be deemed customary where they fall
within the prevailing rate range of regional specialist shops for
equivalent vintage-platform work. Customary-rate determinations shall
not be based on the general-population body-shop rate.

7.3 The insurer may request a shop's published rate schedule prior
to repair authorization. Failure to object to a published schedule
in writing within 14 days of receipt constitutes acknowledgement of
the schedule for purposes of this claim.

## Article IX — Storage

9.1 Reasonable storage at a Specialist Shop is covered up to the
duration reasonably necessary for inspection, estimate, and
disposition. Storage rates published on the shop's customer intake
form, in effect at time of intake, are deemed customary absent
written objection under §7.3.
